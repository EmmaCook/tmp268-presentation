What she actually encounters
👤
First visit: only men in hero imagery on the careers site. Women appear prominently on Instagram and in the live chat. Not here.
✦

❓
To explore roles she must select a profession and entry level upfront. She's not ready to commit. There's no "I'm still exploring" path. And she shouldn't have to submit personal information just to see herself represented.
The inclusive content and the personalisation capability both exist. They are simply not present at the moment of first consideration.

Emma Cook | TMP268 | July 2026Challenge - Commitment-first funnel
Maya - Step 3
Her confirmation email.
Already invisible.
She checks her phone. Nothing in her inbox.

She doesn't think to check spam. Most people don't, especially on a first interaction with a brand they don't know yet.

The first official contact from the Royal Navy never reaches her.
Why it happens
📧
The email is sent from one address but routed through a completely different server. Email providers treat this as a potential fake.
🔓
No email verification step. The system sends the confirmation before checking the address even exists, damaging delivery rates over time.
📂
Generic content, no personalisation, single bold call-to-action. This is exactly the pattern spam filters are built to catch.
For a 16-24 year old checking their phone, a confirmation email in spam is effectively no confirmation email.
Emma Cook | TMP268 | July 2026Challenge - The in-funnel experience
Maya - Step 4
She finds the email.
Then hits another wall.
Maya digs through spam and finds it. She clicks through to the application form.

Nothing is pre-filled. Not her name. Not the answers she gave at registration. Almost every question is one she's already answered once.
What this reveals
📋
The system doesn't remember her at all. Name, contact details, the answers she already gave. All of it asked again from scratch.
💭
"Did they not listen to me? I've already shared this." Two separate systems, no connection between them.
🧭
She's still not sure about her profession answer, and has to face the question again with no more support than the first time.
A system that genuinely remembered her would feel like support. One that asks her to repeat everything feels like nobody was listening.

Emma Cook | TMP268 | July 2026The pivot
Improvement 1
Interest-first.
No commitment required.
I arrived at this because the registration flow has no exploration mode. Every path forces a decision Maya isn't ready to make.

Replace "What's your preferred service?" with a single prompt:
"Curious about the Royal Navy? Leave your email."

No service preference required. No application implied. Just a way in.
Adobe Experience Platform and Adobe Journey Optimizer
What changes at this moment
✉️
A single field. Just an email address. Nothing else required to take the next step.
🚪
The door is open to anyone exploring, not just people ready to commit to a service.
📥
The moment she submits, her interest is captured in Adobe Experience Platform. The relationship begins here, not at registration.
Capturing interest before asking for commitment closes the gap between curiosity and a meaningful first step.

Improvement 1
A 90-second quiz.
Framed as discovery, not screening.
Straight after she leaves her email, Maya is offered something genuinely useful: a short quiz to help her work out where she might fit.

It doesn't feel like an application. It feels like the AI adviser she'll later be offered in her inbox, just a step earlier.

Her answers map her to the Growth Seeker persona. That confidence score lands in Adobe Experience Platform.
Adobe Experience Platform
What the quiz unlocks
🧭
Questions are about motivation, not qualifications. What draws her in, not what she's already decided.
📊
Her result builds a persona confidence score, refined continuously by every email she opens and link she clicks afterward.
🎯
She's matched to Growth Seeker. The system now knows to talk to her about challenge, identity and meaningful change, not qualification levels.
The four personas become the visible architecture of her experience, instead of being invisible logic nobody outside the platform ever sees.

Improvement 2
The highest-intent moment.
Friction-free.
I arrived at this because the data showed the same pattern at every touchpoint after commitment: friction, exactly when trust matters most.

Maya has decided. She's the highest-value prospect in the funnel.

This moment should remove every reason for doubt. Build trust. Create momentum. Confirm she made the right call.
Fixes coordinated with the application IT provider
The fixes
✅
Email verification first. Confirmation only sends to confirmed addresses. Fixes spam delivery and removes bad data downstream.
🔒
Fix the sending setup so confirmation emails come from a verified Royal Navy address. No more spam signals.
📝
Pre-populated application form. Registration data carried forward, editable. Resolves duplicate records too.
🔑
One label. One login. Maya feels ready to be a candidate now. Drop "username" entirely. Refer to candidate ID throughout, from the email to the login screen.

Improvement 3
The content exists.
It's in the wrong place.
I arrived at this by comparing every channel side by side. The representation gap isn't a content problem. It's a distribution problem.

TikTok: rich organic content from women. Unmanaged.
Instagram: women featured. Inclusive tone.
Live chat: strong female representation. Buried as a reactive tool.
Careers site: women almost absent on first visit. This is where Maya decides.
Behavioural signals into personalised content
📍
Adobe Experience Platform tracks Maya's behaviour from the first visit - viewing female officer profiles, dwelling on life-at-sea content, returning within 7 days.
🎯
When her behaviour crosses a threshold, Adobe Journey Optimizer switches the site to show her content that reflects her - peer stories, career continuity FAQs, maternity, physical standards.
💬
Live chat elevated to proactive exploration, and surfaced beyond the website too. It could be linked directly from nurture emails or featured in social content, not just buried on-site.
📐
A CX intervention grounded in behavioural signals. Not a diversity badge. Same mechanism applies to any underrepresented audience.
Emma Cook | TMP268 | July 2026Improvement 3 - Representation gap
Personalisation - Maya is in
She's committed.
Now we need to keep her.
The job is no longer to convince her. It's to keep her.

The gap between passing initial stages and starting Phase One training is where most candidates are lost. Up to a year of waiting, with almost no communication.

At Up Learn, a structured programme mapped to the school year achieved 100% comprehension of key terms by Results Day 2025. The principle applies directly here.
Every stage. Within 24 hours.
⚡
Stage-triggered communications at every step - eligibility, psychometric, medical, fitness, Admiralty Interview Board. Status confirmed, next stage explained, timing set.
🏃
Weekly fitness nudges when a test is 4+ weeks away. Role-appropriate training plan sent automatically. Directly improves pass rates.
🚨
Drop-off detection: 14 days inactive fires a re-engagement email. 28 days flags Maya to a recruiter for a personal call. She is visible before she leaves.
⏳
Biggest opportunity: the gap between medical assessment and the Admiralty Interview Board is the longest, least-supported period. A structured programme here has disproportionate impact.

A/B testing as standard
